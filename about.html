About Suresh:

Suresh is a Ph.D. student at Michigan Technological University's College of Computing, specializing in computational biology, machine learning, and computer vision. He works as a Graduate Research Assistant at KC-Lab, where he is developing deep learning models to locate various post-translational modifications in protein sequences.

Suresh earned a Bachelor of Computer Engineering and an M.Sc. in Computer Systems and Knowledge Engineering from Tribhuvan University, Nepal. During his master's, he gained experience as an instructor for programming languages, database management systems, machine learning, and image processing courses.

For his undergraduate research project, Suresh designed a signature recognition system using a Convolutional Neural Network and transfer learning. His graduate-level thesis involved developing a Scale Invariant Feature Transform (SIFT) based Image Quality Assessment (IQA) technique to evaluate the quality of High-Resolution images generated by generative adversarial networks.

With several years of experience as a software engineer, Suresh has a practical approach to research problems. He brings a diverse skill set to his work, allowing him to approach problems creatively and efficiently.

Overall, Suresh's academic and professional background, combined with his enthusiasm for computational biology, machine learning, and computer vision, make him a promising Ph.D. candidate and a valuable asset to the research community. His strong technical skills and practical approach to research enable him to tackle complex problems and develop innovative solutions.
